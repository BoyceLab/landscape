# About

## About this resource

The Boyce Lab Genetic Epilepsy Landscape is a community-facing reference resource compiled and maintained by the Boyce Lab. It started as background research for a talk to the KCNT1 epilepsy patient community and grew into a broader effort to map the patient infrastructure, research ecosystem, and therapeutic development pipeline across all monogenic epilepsy genes.

The work is intentionally framed as **co-production** — built from the outset with the rare epilepsy community in mind, designed to be improved by community input, and shared so anyone can reuse it.

## About the Boyce Lab

[Boyce Lab](https://boycedatascience.com/) is an independent data science consultancy run by Danielle Boyce, focused on translating complex data into resources that help patient communities, foundations, and researchers move faster.

## Submitting corrections or additions

The fastest way to suggest a change is the **+ Suggest a correction** button at the top of the [dashboard](dashboard.md). It opens a small form where you can specify the type of submission (correction, addition, question, or other), the gene or topic, your message, and optionally your name, email, and affiliation. Submitting opens your email client with a pre-filled, structured message — review and send when you're ready.

The form is privacy-preserving by design: no backend, no tracking, no data leaves your browser until you choose to send the email yourself.

## What kind of submissions help most

**Corrections** — A foundation URL changed; a registry platform was misnamed; a therapeutic program advanced or was discontinued; a research center moved.

**Additions** — A new foundation we missed; a registry that just launched; a clinical trial that started; a therapeutic milestone (FDA approval, first patient dosed, etc.); a community we should know about.

**Questions** — Are we surfacing the right information? Is the framing of a particular gene's status fair? Is something confusing or missing context?

**Other** — Anything that would make this a better resource for your community.

## How updates flow

Submitted corrections are reviewed individually. The landscape is rebuilt and republished after meaningful batches of changes accumulate, or when Genes4Epilepsy publishes a new release (twice yearly). We aim to acknowledge every submission, though sometimes the response is a slow nod rather than an immediate update.
