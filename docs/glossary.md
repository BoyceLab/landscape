# Glossary

## Status tiers

<span class="status-badge status-established">●●● Established</span> Active patient foundation + research infrastructure

<span class="status-badge status-emerging">●●○ Emerging</span> Partial or growing infrastructure

<span class="status-badge status-background">●○○ Background</span> Gene confirmed in epilepsy; no organized community identified

## Registry platforms

The dashboard's Registry Platform(s) column uses the following canonical names. A gene's stack often includes 2–5 platforms in parallel — multi-platform-by-design is the maturity signal at the Established tier.

[NORD IAMRARE](https://rarediseases.org/iamrare-registry-program/)
:   The National Organization for Rare Disorders' registry-as-a-service platform for rare disease foundations.

[RARE-X / Global Genes](https://globalgenes.org/rare-x/)
:   Centralized data hub. RARE-X merged into Global Genes in 2023.

[Simons Searchlight (SFARI Base)](https://www.simonssearchlight.org/)
:   The SFARI / Simons Foundation NDD-focused research registry. Approximately 156 single genes plus 24 CNVs as of 2024–2026.

[Sanford CoRDS](https://research.sanfordhealth.org/rare-disease-registry)
:   Coordination of Rare Diseases at Sanford — a generic rare-disease registry that accepts any rare disease patient. Free and patient-driven.

[Matrix (Across Healthcare)](https://www.acrossmatrix.com/)
:   Across Healthcare's white-label registry and patient records platform. Used by 85+ rare disease organizations across 180 conditions in 121 countries. Often deployed under foundation-specific branding, so the platform may not be named publicly.

[Pulse Infoframe](https://pulseinfoframe.com/)
:   Commercial registry platform (healthie 2.0). Powers some foundation registries through the Invitae Patient Insight Network.

[Citizen Health](https://www.citizen.health/)
:   Patient-led data partnership platform. Used by CHD2, DNM1, SLC6A1, SYNGAP1, and others.

Academic / REDCap
:   University-hosted custom registries on REDCap or similar platforms. Examples: ICDD (CDKL5, U Western Australia), RIKEE (KCNQ2/3), InterRett (Rett), TOSCA (TSC). Often the most scientifically detailed but least patient-facing.

[Combined Brain biorepository](https://www.combinedbrain.org/)
:   A consortium of 25+ rare neurodevelopmental disorder foundations operating shared biorepository and outcome measure work (including the FDA-funded ORCA communication tool). Increasingly central to clinical-trial readiness for the field.

Foundation-hosted (custom)
:   Proprietary registry built by the foundation itself, not on a shared platform.

[ClinicalTrials.gov NH study](https://clinicaltrials.gov/)
:   Formal NIH-listed natural history study, often industry-sponsored.

[NIH RDCRN](https://www.rarediseasesnetwork.org/)
:   Rare Diseases Clinical Research Network — federally funded multi-site disease consortia. Examples in this landscape: NAMDC (mitochondrial), RSNH (Rett), FCDGC (CDG), UBDRC (Batten), ALLFTD (FTD/GRN), AP-4-HSP (Boston Children's).

## Common abbreviations

DEE
:   Developmental and Epileptic Encephalopathy

NDD
:   Neurodevelopmental Disorder

GGE
:   Genetic Generalized Epilepsy (older term: IGE — idiopathic generalized epilepsy)

MCD
:   Malformation of Cortical Development

FE
:   Focal Epilepsy

NCL
:   Neuronal Ceroid Lipofuscinosis (Batten disease family)

CDG
:   Congenital Disorders of Glycosylation

PME
:   Progressive Myoclonic Epilepsy

SUDEP
:   Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilepsy

GoF / LoF
:   Gain-of-function / Loss-of-function — the variant's functional consequence, often distinguishing therapeutic strategies for the same gene

ASO
:   Antisense Oligonucleotide — a class of precision genetic medicine

AAV
:   Adeno-Associated Virus — common vector for gene replacement therapy

NH (study)
:   Natural History study — longitudinal observation of untreated disease course, foundational for trial design

## Inheritance abbreviations

AD
:   Autosomal Dominant

AR
:   Autosomal Recessive

XL
:   X-Linked

XLR / XLD
:   X-linked Recessive / X-linked Dominant

mtDNA
:   Mitochondrially encoded

de novo
:   Variant arose in the affected individual rather than inherited

## Tier-1 reference foundations frequently mentioned

These come up repeatedly because they cover multiple genes or set models others follow.

CDKL5 — IFCR / Loulou
:   The reference standard mature DEE ecosystem; first FDA-approved gene-specific DEE therapy (ganaxolone, 2022)

Dravet Syndrome Foundation (SCN1A)
:   Most mature DEE foundation overall; multiple FDA-approved therapies and multiple late-stage precision medicine trials

ASF / FAST (UBE3A / Angelman)
:   Most advanced clinical pipeline among monogenic NDDs (Phase 3 ASO, Phase 1/2 AAV)

Rettsyndrome.org / RSRT / Reverse Rett (MECP2)
:   Reference standard for rare NDD organization at scale

TSC Alliance
:   One of the largest, oldest, and best-funded rare disease ecosystems in epilepsy; covers TSC1+TSC2 jointly

CureGRIN
:   Umbrella covering GRIN1, GRIN2A, GRIN2B, GRIN2D, GRIA1-4, GRID, GRIK — a rare cross-gene model

BDSRA / Beyond Batten
:   Umbrella covering all CLN-family NCL genes (CLN1–14) — another cross-gene model
