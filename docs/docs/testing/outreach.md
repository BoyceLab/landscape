# Outreach templates

Recruitment and follow-up emails for five audiences — PAG leadership, patients and families, clinicians, academic researchers, industry researchers — plus scheduling, reminders, thank-yous, and a results share-back.

## How to use

Every template here is a starting point. Bracketed placeholders like `[PROJECT NAME]`, `[SITE URL]`, `[DATE]`, and `[YOUR NAME]` need to be filled in before sending. Beyond that, rewrite at least the opening line to sound like you. Recipients can tell when a message has been copy-pasted unedited, and personalization is the difference between a yes and a polite no.

Tone notes follow each template. Use them to decide what to adjust if your recipient is slightly different from the audience the template was written for.

!!! tip "Tips that apply to every audience"
    - Keep the first email under 200 words. Long pitches signal that you'll be a heavy ask.
    - Lead with what you want, not your project's history. Respect their scan-reading.
    - Be specific about time commitment up front — 60–90 minutes is the answer to the question they're already wondering.
    - Make it easy to decline. "No worries if not" beats no exit ramp at all.

## 1. Patient advocacy group leadership

For executive directors, founders, or program leads at patient foundations. They are gatekeepers and connectors — even if they can't test themselves, they can refer staff or community members.

### Initial outreach

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Quick favor: testing a new resource for [CONDITION AREA] communities

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    I hope you're well. I'm reaching out because we've been building [PROJECT NAME] — [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION] — and we'd really value your perspective before we share it more widely.

    We're looking for a small group of people to spend 60–90 minutes with us walking through the site, telling us what's confusing, what's missing, and whether it reflects the field accurately. Your foundation's work is part of what's represented, and we want to be sure we've gotten it right.

    Two ways you could help:

    1. Sit with us yourself for a session — your view of the landscape is exactly the kind of feedback we need.
    2. Pass this along to a staff member or community member who you think would be a good fit.

    Either is welcome — or both. We're happy to share the resource with you in advance if that helps you decide.

    Sessions happen [DATE RANGE]. We can work around your schedule. Just reply and let me know if you're open to it, or if there's someone else on your team I should reach.

    Thanks for considering — and for everything your foundation does for this community.

    Warmly,
    [YOUR NAME]
    [ROLE / AFFILIATION]

*Tone notes: Warm and community-oriented. Acknowledges their authority and time. Offers two ways to help so a "yes" is easy. The line about "we want to be sure we've gotten it right" matters — foundation leaders are wary of being misrepresented and will respond to that signal.*

### When they offer to refer someone

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Thank you — and a quick handoff

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Thank you, that's very generous. Could you forward the note below to [NAME OF REFERRAL or "whoever you think is the best fit"]? I'm happy to take it from there.

    Really appreciate the connection.

    [YOUR NAME]

    ---

    [FORWARDABLE VERSION: a short third-person intro they can drop into an email — e.g., "[Your name] is testing a new resource for the [condition area] community and would love 60–90 minutes of your time. Background and what they're looking for is below."]

## 2. Patients and family members

Almost always reached via a foundation or community connection. This template is designed to be sent directly or forwarded by a foundation contact.

!!! note "Special considerations"
    - Many recipients are exhausted, grieving, or stretched thin. Acknowledge that without making it the centerpiece.
    - Plain language only. No "phenotype," "infrastructure," or "landscape."
    - Be very clear about what they will and won't be asked. Anxiety about "what does this involve" is a major drop-off point.
    - Compensation matters here in a way it may not for clinicians — patient and family time is rarely paid, and offering it signals respect.

### Initial outreach (sent directly)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Would you spend an hour helping shape a resource for families like yours?

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    [CONNECTION CONTEXT — e.g., "[Foundation name] suggested I reach out" or "We met briefly at [event]"].

    I'm part of a small team putting together a website that helps people find resources, communities, and research for genetic conditions — including [CONDITION]. Before we share it more widely, we want to know whether it actually works for families. That means asking real parents and family members to look at it and tell us honestly what's helpful, what's confusing, and what's missing.

    Would you have about an hour to spend with me, over a video call, looking at the site and giving me your reactions? You don't need to prepare anything. There are no right answers. I'm testing the website, not you.

    A few specifics:

    - About 60 to 90 minutes total
    - A video call at a time that works for you
    - [COMPENSATION DETAIL — e.g., "A $75 thank-you gift card"]
    - Your name won't appear anywhere; I'll just describe you as "a parent" if I share what I learned

    If you're open to it, just reply and we'll find a time. If not, no worries at all — I know how much you have on your plate already.

    Thank you for thinking about it.

    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: The warmest in the toolkit. No jargon. Front-loads what they're being asked for. The privacy line addresses a real concern families have about being identifiable. The closing "no worries at all" matters — families say yes out of obligation otherwise.*

### Initial outreach (forwarded by a foundation contact)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** A short note from [FOUNDATION NAME]

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    I'm passing along a note from [YOUR NAME AND ROLE], who's working on a new website resource for the [CONDITION] community. They're looking for parents and family members to spend an hour helping make sure it actually works for people like us. I know your time is precious — this is a gentle ask, not a pressured one.

    If you're open, you can reach them directly at [EMAIL]. If not, no need to do anything.

    Warmly,
    [FOUNDATION CONTACT NAME]

    ---

    [Original message from YOUR NAME follows below, optionally lightly trimmed to remove the connection-context line.]

*Tone notes: When a trusted foundation contact forwards an ask, response rates jump. Make this version available so they can send it themselves without rewriting.*

## 3. Clinicians

Neurologists, geneticists, genetic counselors. Their time is the most fragmented of any audience; concision is mandatory.

### Initial outreach

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** 60–90 min: test a new clinical reference for [CONDITION AREA]

    Dr. [LAST NAME],

    I'm writing to ask for 60–90 minutes of your time. We've built [PROJECT NAME] — [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION] — and before broader release, we'd value an honest read from a practicing clinician.

    What we'd ask: a video call where you look at the site, react out loud, and try a handful of realistic lookups (e.g., pulling resources for a newly diagnosed patient with a specific gene variant). No prep, no homework.

    What we offer: [HONORARIUM AMOUNT, if applicable], and the resource itself will be free and openly accessible.

    Sessions are running [DATE RANGE]. Reply with two or three windows that work and I'll send a calendar invite.

    Thank you for considering,
    [YOUR NAME], [DEGREE / ROLE]
    [AFFILIATION]

*Tone notes: Concise, no-frills. Leads with the time ask. Use "Dr." unless you're peers. The line about a "realistic lookup" tells them what kind of cognitive load they're signing up for.*

### Cold outreach (no prior connection)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Brief request from [AFFILIATION] — clinician input on a reference resource

    Dr. [LAST NAME],

    Apologies for the cold note. I'm reaching out because [SPECIFIC REASON — e.g., "your work on [TOPIC] makes you exactly the kind of clinician who would use this resource"].

    We've built [PROJECT NAME] — [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION] — and we're looking for clinicians to spend 60–90 minutes giving us honest feedback before broader release.

    If you're open to it, reply with a couple of windows that could work and I'll handle the rest. If not, I understand completely.

    [YOUR NAME], [DEGREE / ROLE]
    [AFFILIATION]

*Tone notes: For cold outreach, the "specific reason" line is the difference between getting read and getting filtered. Reference something concrete — a paper, a clinical area, a known affiliation — not a generic "your expertise."*

## 4. Academic researchers and Scientific Advisory Board

Peer-to-peer tone. They will read this as colleagues.

### Initial outreach (existing relationship)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Could I borrow you for 90 minutes? Testing [PROJECT NAME]

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Hope research is going well. I'm writing to ask if you'd be willing to spend 60–90 minutes user-testing [PROJECT NAME] before we release it more broadly.

    Quick context: [PROJECT NAME] is [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]. We want to know whether the structure, classifications, and source attribution stand up to scrutiny from someone who works in the space.

    The session would be a video call where I'd ask you to react to the site cold, walk through a few realistic lookups ("find me a condition where X is true"), and tell me what's missing or miscategorized. No prep on your part.

    Sessions are running [DATE RANGE]. Happy to work around you. Let me know if you're open and I'll send some windows.

    Thanks,
    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Peer-to-peer, can use technical terms. The phrase "stand up to scrutiny from someone who works in the space" appeals to their identity as a critical reader — and warns them that you actually want criticism.*

### SAB member specifically

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** An SAB-adjacent ask: 90 min user-testing [PROJECT NAME]

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Bringing a small, time-bounded ask. We've built [PROJECT NAME] and want SAB-quality feedback before broader release. Specifically: does the structure represent the field accurately, are the classifications defensible, and is the sourcing transparent enough to cite?

    60–90 minutes, video call, no prep. I'd walk you through it and capture your reactions.

    If you have time in [DATE RANGE], reply with a few windows and I'll get something on the calendar.

    Thanks,
    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Shorter. Frames the ask as separate from SAB duties ("SAB-adjacent") so they know it isn't replacing or augmenting their standing commitments. The three explicit questions tell them you want substance, not validation.*

## 5. Industry researchers (biotech / pharma)

Scientists at biotech or pharma companies, typically in rare disease, gene therapy, or the specific therapeutic area covered. Their motivation is often pragmatic: does this resource help them do their job?

### Initial outreach

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Brief test of a landscape resource — your perspective would help

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    I'm writing because the resource we've built — [PROJECT NAME] — would plausibly be used by people in roles like yours, and we'd value your input before broader release.

    [PROJECT NAME] is [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]. If you've ever needed to scope a therapeutic area, identify community partners, or check whether a registry exists for a given gene, this is aimed at making that faster.

    We're looking for 60–90 minutes: a video call where you'd react to the site, run a few realistic lookups, and tell us what's useful, what's missing, and what's miscategorized from your vantage point.

    [HONORARIUM AMOUNT, or note that the resource itself will be free / openly accessible].

    Open to it? Reply with a few windows in [DATE RANGE] and I'll set something up.

    [YOUR NAME]
    [AFFILIATION]

*Tone notes: Practical, value-prop forward. The line about "scope a therapeutic area, identify community partners, or check whether a registry exists" tells them exactly what the resource could save them time on.*

## Follow-up templates

These apply across audiences. Adjust tone using the notes above.

### Scheduling confirmation (after they say yes)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Confirmed: [DATE/TIME] — looking forward to it

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Thank you for saying yes. Confirming we're set for [DATE AND TIME, with time zone].

    Video link: [LINK]
    Backup phone: [NUMBER, in case the video fails]

    I'm attaching a short welcome packet that walks through what we'll do. It's about a five-minute read. Couple of things to know in advance:

    - Please don't explore the site before the session — first impressions are part of what we want to capture.
    - Have your phone or tablet handy; we'll do a quick mobile check.
    - If you'd like to decline being recorded, that's fine — just let me know at the start.

    Reply with any questions. See you [DAY OF WEEK].

    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: The three bulleted items reduce day-of friction. Link the [welcome packet](welcome-packet.md) here.*

### Reminder (24 hours before)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Tomorrow at [TIME]: [PROJECT NAME] session

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Quick reminder we're meeting tomorrow at [TIME, TIME ZONE]. Video link is below.

    [LINK]

    If anything has come up and you need to reschedule, just reply — no problem.

    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Very short. The reschedule offer is genuine — a tester who shows up resentful gives worse feedback than one who reschedules.*

### Thank you (immediately after the session)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Thank you — and one small follow-up

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Thank you for the time today. Your reactions were genuinely useful — especially [SPECIFIC THING THEY SAID OR DID, e.g., "the way you described the difference between Established and Emerging when you first saw the legend — that's something we're going to act on"].

    One small follow-up: when you have a moment in the next day or two, could you fill out this short feedback form? It captures the things that often surface after a session, once you've had a chance to reflect.

    [FORM LINK]

    [COMPENSATION NEXT STEP — e.g., "I'll send the gift card by end of week."]

    Thanks again,
    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Personalize the second sentence with something they actually said. It tells them they were heard, and it makes the form much more likely to get completed.*

### Form reminder (if no response after 3 days)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Friendly nudge: feedback form for [PROJECT NAME]

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Just a gentle nudge — I sent over a short feedback form after our session and wanted to make sure it didn't get buried. It takes about 10 minutes.

    [FORM LINK]

    No pressure if you'd rather skip it — what you shared in the session is already enormously helpful. But if you have the time, it would be a real contribution.

    Thanks,
    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Send once. If they don't respond, don't send a second.*

### Results share-back (when revisions are complete)

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** What changed because of your feedback

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    I wanted to circle back. Based on your session and the others we ran, we made the following changes to [PROJECT NAME]:

    - [CHANGE 1 — preferably one this specific tester raised]
    - [CHANGE 2]
    - [CHANGE 3]

    The updated site is live at [URL] if you want to take a look. You don't need to do anything — I just wanted you to see that your feedback actually shifted things.

    Thanks again,
    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: This is the email most projects skip — and it's the one that builds long-term goodwill. Testers remember being heard. Lead with a change they specifically raised.*

### Polite decline acknowledgment

!!! example "Email"
    **Subject:** Thanks for letting me know

    Hi [FIRST NAME],

    Totally understood — thanks for replying. If timing is the issue and a future window would work better, just let me know. Otherwise no worries at all.

    [YOUR NAME]

*Tone notes: Always reply to a decline, briefly. It keeps the door open for a future cycle.*

## Recruitment tracking

Track responses in a simple list. Minimum useful columns:

| Name | Audience | Sent | Replied | Scheduled | Completed | Form returned |
|------|----------|------|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
|      |          |      |         |           |           |               |

Aim for a 50–70% conversion rate from "replied yes" to "completed session." If your rate is much lower, the welcome packet or the time commitment may be scaring people off — revisit before sending more outreach.
