# Research readiness maturity model

Most landscapes describe what exists. This one also describes a path. Every rare epilepsy organization moves, at its own pace, from an informal community toward sharing standardized data that outlives any single group. The maturity model names the stages on that path so an organization can see where it stands, where it is ahead, and where it is behind.

This model replaces the earlier status tiers. The tiers described observable infrastructure from the outside. This model describes an organization's own trajectory, and it is populated by self-assessment, not assigned from outside.

[Take the self-assessment](self-assessment.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary } [Open the interactive model](atlas.md){ .md-button }

## The five stages

An organization lands in one of five overall stages based on where it sits across the tracks below.

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

- __1. Emerging__

    An informal community exists. No legal or research infrastructure.

- __2. Organized__

    Incorporated and governed. Beginning to think about research.

- __3. Connected__

    Plugged into the broader research ecosystem. Collecting structured information about patients.

- __4. Data Generating__

    Operating registries or natural history infrastructure. Producing data with intent.

- __5. Data Sharing__

    Contributing standardized, documented data or metadata to shared or open resources for use beyond the org.

</div>

!!! note "How the composite level works"
    An org's composite level is roughly the lowest stage it has reliably reached across the core tracks. You cannot be Data Sharing overall if you have no governance. The tracks show exactly where an org is ahead or behind.

## The five tracks

Each track runs through the same five stages. Reading across a track shows an organization what good looks like one step ahead, and the kind of support that tends to help at each step.

### Organizational Foundation

**Stage 1 — Informal community.** Do we have a space where patients and families find each other? Does someone informally keep things going?  
*Needs support with:* Turning a loose group into something durable; understanding what becoming an organization involves.  
*How the network helps:* Plain language orientation on nonprofit formation; Connect founders to peer organizations who have made the leap

**Stage 2 — Unincorporated association.** Do we have identified leaders, even without legal status? Do we have a shared mission written down?  
*Needs support with:* The legal and administrative mechanics of incorporation; drafting governing documents.  
*How the network helps:* Pro bono legal services for incorporation and bylaws; Governance templates; Board recruitment guidance

**Stage 3 — Incorporated nonprofit.** Are we a registered nonprofit with bylaws and a functioning board? Can we legally accept donations and enter agreements?  
*Needs support with:* Moving from legally exists to actually operating; first strategic plan; early fundraising.  
*How the network helps:* Strategic planning facilitation; Grant writing support; Nonprofit financial and accounting services

**Stage 4 — Staffed and resourced.** Do we have paid staff or a dedicated contractor? Do we have a strategic plan and a research budget line?  
*Needs support with:* Building research capacity; hiring the right roles; sustainable funding.  
*How the network helps:* Research operations consulting; Advice on hiring a research lead; Introductions to research funders

**Stage 5 — Sustainable.** Do we have diversified funding and someone leading research? Could the org survive its founder's departure?  
*Needs support with:* Succession, long term sustainability, scaling impact.  
*How the network helps:* Executive coaching and succession planning; Endowment or reserve building strategy; Governance maturity review

### Scientific and Community Engagement

**Stage 1 — Peer support.** Do members share experiences and information?  
*Needs support with:* Recognizing the community's value as a research asset.  
*How the network helps:* Educate the community on how patient experience informs research; Introduce the concept of patient reported data

**Stage 2 — Awareness and connecting.** Do we help families find clinicians or trials? Do we do awareness raising beyond our members?  
*Needs support with:* Identifying which clinicians and researchers work on their condition.  
*How the network helps:* Clinician and researcher landscape mapping; Trial finder navigation support

**Stage 3 — Priorities from community.** Have we asked our community what research is important to them? Do we have a group that channels patient input into priorities?  
*Needs support with:* Running a credible priority setting process.  
*How the network helps:* Facilitate a research priority setting exercise (James Lind Alliance style); Survey design

**Stage 4 — Advisory board.** Do we have a scientific or medical advisory board that meets and influences decisions?  
*Needs support with:* Recruiting credible experts; running an effective board.  
*How the network helps:* Serve on or help recruit the advisory board; Advise on charters and conflict of interest policy

**Stage 5 — Agenda setting.** Do we help define the research agenda for our condition? Have researchers co-designed studies with us?  
*Needs support with:* Sustaining genuine co-design as the org scales.  
*How the network helps:* Partner as a co-investigator; Build patient engagement into grant proposals as a named partner

### Ecosystem Integration

**Stage 1 — Isolated.** Are we essentially working on our own?  
*Needs support with:* Knowing the ecosystem exists and how to enter it.  
*How the network helps:* Introductions to consortia and umbrella organizations; An ecosystem map

**Stage 2 — Informal ties.** Do we have a relationship with at least one researcher or clinician?  
*Needs support with:* Converting a single relationship into broader engagement.  
*How the network helps:* Warm introductions to relevant consortia (REN, CombinedBrain, NORD)

**Stage 3 — Consortium member.** Are we an active member of a consortium or umbrella?  
*Needs support with:* Getting real value from membership, not just belonging.  
*How the network helps:* Advise on how to participate actively; Identify collaboration opportunities within the consortium

**Stage 4 — Active collaborations.** Are we in shared projects with other organizations?  
*Needs support with:* Managing multi party projects and data sharing across orgs.  
*How the network helps:* Project management support; Multi site agreement and data governance templates

**Stage 5 — Recognized node.** Do others come to us as a partner or convener?  
*Needs support with:* The load of convening; staying strategic.  
*How the network helps:* Infrastructure or staffing to support a convening role; Co-host workshops

### Data Infrastructure

**Stage 1 — No structured data.** Is most of what we know anecdotal or scattered?  
*Needs support with:* Understanding what structured data is and its importance.  
*How the network helps:* A plain language primer on registries; Help define what data would be worth collecting

**Stage 2 — Contact registry.** Do we have a maintained list of who our patients are and how to reach them?  
*Needs support with:* Moving from a contact list to research data; privacy basics.  
*How the network helps:* Advise on consent for re-contact; Help select a registry platform if they decide they want that

**Stage 3 — Patient reported registry.** Do we collect structured patient reported data on a real platform, with meaningful enrollment?  
*Needs support with:* Survey and instrument quality; boosting enrollment and retention.  
*How the network helps:* Instrument design and validation; Recruitment strategy; Biostatistics consultation

**Stage 4 — Natural history and longitudinal.** Are we collecting data over time with a defined data model?  
*Needs support with:* Clinical data collection, longitudinal follow up, data quality.  
*How the network helps:* Natural history study design; Clinical data management; REDCap or equivalent setup

**Stage 5 — Harmonized to standards.** Is our data mapped to common standards and combinable with other datasets?  
*Needs support with:* Mapping to common data elements; interoperability.  
*How the network helps:* Data harmonization services; Ontology and common data element mapping; Informatics support

### Data Governance and Sharing · the north star

**Stage 1 — No governance.** Are we holding data without a clear consent or privacy framework?  
*Needs support with:* Basic consent, privacy, and ethics; understanding obligations.  
*How the network helps:* Draft research consent and privacy policies; IRB and ethics navigation

**Stage 2 — Internal governance.** Do we have consent and privacy policies, with data held internally?  
*Needs support with:* Preparing data to be intelligible and shareable beyond the org.  
*How the network helps:* Review consent for whether it permits future sharing; Introduce data sharing concepts

**Stage 3 — Documented and standardized.** Do we have a published data dictionary and standardized variables? Could an outsider understand our dataset from the documentation alone?  
*Needs support with:* Writing and publishing a data dictionary; standardizing variables.  
*How the network helps:* Build the data dictionary; Map variables to common data elements; Documentation support

**Stage 4 — Shared with a partner.** Have we shared data with a vetted partner under a formal agreement (Data for the Common Good, RDCA-DAP)? Do we have a governance policy for how sharing decisions get made?  
*Needs support with:* Data sharing agreements; a governance process for access requests.  
*How the network helps:* Draft data sharing and use agreements; Set up a data access committee; Broker partner relationships

**Stage 5 — Open and broadly accessible.** Is our data or metadata findable by researchers beyond named partners, meeting FAIR principles? Can qualified researchers discover and request it through a defined process?  
*Needs support with:* Making data FAIR; sustaining open access responsibly.  
*How the network helps:* FAIR-ification services; Deposit into recognized repositories; Publish discoverable metadata even when raw data stays controlled
